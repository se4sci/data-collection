19         text="""
20     This parameter applies a broadening to the spectrum and is used to avoid
21     divergences in the sum-over-states approach.
22     The sum-over-states approach to the linear and nonlinear susceptibilities
23     inevitably include resonant denominators of the form
24     $(\omega - \omega_{nm})^{-1}$, see for example Eq. 46 of [[cite:Sharma2004]].
25     Numerically these denominators lead to infinities. In order to avoid them
26     one could do one of two things.
27     One could change the sum over k-points to integration, and then use the
28     linear tetrahedron method (see [[cite:Hughes1996]] for details).
29     Another way to get around the problem, as we will do in the present case,
30     is to avoid the singularities by adding a small imaginary contribution to the
31     denominator. This addition prevents the denominator from ever going to 0,
32     and acts as a broadening to the spectrum. The broadening should not be too
33     large, as this would wash out the features in the spectrum.
34     """,
