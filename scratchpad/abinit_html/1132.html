62     More detailed explanations: the total energy should be invariant under
63     translation of the crystal as a whole. This would guarantee that the three
64     lowest phonon modes at Gamma have zero frequency (Acoustic Sum Rule - ASR).
65     Unfortunately, the way the DDB is generated (presence of a discrete grid of
66     points for the evaluation of the exchange-correlation potential and energy)
67     slightly breaks the translational invariance. Well, in some pathological
68     cases, the breaking can be rather important.
69     
70     Two quantities are affected: the interatomic forces (or dynamical matrices),
71     and the effective charges. The ASR for the effective charges is called the
72     charge neutrality sum rule, and will be dealt with by the variable
73     [[anaddb:chneut]]. The ASR for the interatomic forces can be restored, by
74     modifying the interatomic force of the atom on itself, (called self-IFC), as
75     soon as the dynamical matrix at Gamma is known. This quantity should be equal
76     to minus the sum of all interatomic forces generated by all others atoms
77     (action-reaction law!), which is determined by the dynamical matrix at Gamma.
78     
79     So, if **asr** is non-zero, the correction to the self-force will be
80     determined, and the self-force will be imposed to be consistent with the ASR.
81     This correction will work if IFCs are computed ([[anaddb:ifcflag]]/=0), as
82     well as if the IFCs are not computed ([[anaddb:ifcflag]]==0). In both cases,
83     the phonon frequencies will not be the same as the ones determined by the
84     output of abinit, RF case. If you want to check that the DDB is correct, by
85     comparing phonon frequencies from abinit and anaddb, you should turn off both
86     **asr** and [[anaddb:chneut]].
87     
88     Until now, we have not explained the difference between **asr** =1 and **asr**
89     =2. This is rather subtle. In some local low-symmetry cases (basically the
90     effective charges should be anisotropic), when the dipole-dipole contribution
91     is evaluated and subtracted, the ASR cannot be imposed without breaking the
92     symmetry of the on-site interatomic forces. That explains why two options are
93     given: the second case ( **asr** =2, sym) does not entirely impose the ASR,
94     but simply the part that keeps the on-site interatomic forces symmetric (which
95     means that the acoustic frequencies do not go to zero exactly), the first case
96     ( **asr** =1, asym) imposes the ASR, but breaks the symmetry. **asr** =2 is to
97     be preferred for the analysis of the interatomic force constant in real space,
98     while **asr** =1 should be used to get the phonon band structure.
